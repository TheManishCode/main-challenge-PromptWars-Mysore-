# Product

## Register

product

## Users

High-stakes exam aspirants in India (NEET, JEE, CUET, CAT, GATE, UPSC, board exams), typically 16–24. They reach for MindTrail when they are stressed, tired, behind, or quietly panicking — often late at night, often on a phone, often after a study session that didn't go well. Their context is high pressure and low spare attention. They do not have the energy to fill out long forms or learn an interface.

## Product Purpose

A private daily companion that helps a student notice how they're really doing, lower the pressure in the moment, and keep moving on their preparation without burning out. It pairs lightweight self-tracking (mood, sleep, journal) with an AI companion that listens and asks good questions, surfaces hidden stress patterns, and offers small concrete next steps. Success is a student who, on a hard day, opens MindTrail and leaves a little calmer and one small step less stuck — not more behind.

## Brand Personality

Warm, human, steady. A supportive friend who happens to understand exam pressure — not a clinician, not a hype coach, not a productivity drill sergeant. Voice is plain, kind, and specific: it names feelings honestly, never lectures, never catastrophizes a casual hello. Three words: **warm, calm, encouraging.**

## Anti-references

- **Cold / clinical.** No hospital-white sterility, no medical-intake-form energy, nothing that makes an anxious student feel like a patient or a case file.
- **Busy / cluttered.** No dense dashboards, no wall of competing widgets, no high cognitive load. An overwhelmed person should never feel more overwhelmed here.
- Also avoid: corporate-SaaS hero-metric templates, identical card grids, and childish over-gamification (confetti, loud badges) that patronizes serious aspirants.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm is a feature, not a coat of paint.** Generous space, one clear thing per screen, soft motion. The interface should physically lower the user's shoulders.
2. **Lowest possible activation energy.** A heavy day should still get a check-in. Always offer the 10-second path before the full one.
3. **Honest warmth, never hype.** Encourage with specifics grounded in the student's real data; never fake cheer, never minimize a hard day.
4. **Progress over pressure.** Reflect effort, rest, and small wins — not streak-shaming or grind-glorification. Rest counts as progress.
5. **The tool disappears into the task.** Familiar, trustworthy controls; consistent vocabulary; delight saved for moments, not sprayed across pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, placeholders included. Full keyboard operability, visible focus rings, semantic landmarks and labels. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere (calm crossfades instead of movement). Multilingual support for Indian languages already present in chat/voice must be preserved. Touch targets ≥44px; phone-first layouts. Never rely on color alone to carry meaning (mood/burnout states pair color with text).
